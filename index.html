Case Study

The Case Study is set to test the participants economical and entrepreneurial skills to the fullest. The debate will be very informative and shall provide a plat-form for budding entrepreneurs to express their ideas and test their knowledge. The crisis would be emailed to the head participants and uploaded on the website 3 days prior to the Event. Participants are requested to go through the case study thoroughly in order to find solutions to the problems presented. Two members of each team will be required to participate in the Cast Study.

Event Format

Round 1

The discussions being simulated will be relating to a firm that will either be fac-ing an economic crisis or the market will be in a state of recession. Each team shall be allotted a member of the Board of Governors of the aforementioned firm and shall have to work in unison to formulate a plan for the revival of the firm as well as a method to minimise loss and maximise profit.There shall be two sessions of the initial round. The participants will be in a state of constant debate with a head Entrepreneur guiding them; Constant updates on the crisis shall be provided to keep the delegate fresh.The participants shall be judged solely on their contribution to the solving of the given crisis. At the end of the crisis a shortlist of the top teams shall be formulated with the top eight teams progressing to the next round. These teams shall be given there allotments along with the details for the final round of the crisis.

Round 2

The round shall begin with a briefing of the situation. This round shall simulate a situation of national recession with crashing markets and financial pandemo-nium. Each of the teams shall be allotted firms that control various markets of the economy and they would have to devise a proper solution to boost the fail-ing economy.The participants shall be required to form a concrete resolution on how they plan to go about the crisis and they will have to discuss how to solve the current one as well as how to prevent a new one. The session shall once again have a guiding body whose head shall be the president of the econ-omy in crisis. This is to keep the participants on track during the session as time will be of the essence. Furthermore all this shall have to be done by the end of the session by which the solution shall be put into effect and the econo-my shall then in unison be crossing the point of no return. The participants shall be judged on their contribution to the crisis as well as their displayed entrepreneurial skill and knowledge of modern economics.

Ad Making

The Advertisement Making Competition is a competition that has featured in every edition of the previously held Aitchison Business Concepts. This clearly shows the importance of their being such a competition in the upcoming one. The Media has become a major part of our lives and moreover so firms have continued to benefit from it, how? Through advertisements, and so we believe the participants should be tested with such a competition.

Event Format

Round 1

At the time of registration each team shall be allotted a company. The compa-ny may be local or international. The basic gist is to make an innovative and creative advertisement for said company and present it in the first round.

The rules are as follows:

• Each Advertisement must be 55-70 seconds long.

• The Advertisement must not degrade any rival product; only promote the assigned one.

• After the Advertisement has been played the team shall be subjected to a 1-minute question and answer session including questions from both the judges and the audience.

• The Advertisement may not contain any foul material or language or any crude or vulgar displays.

• The advertisements may not contain any material used in ay previously held competition or marks shall be cut for plagiarism.

After the end of the round each team shall be treated to an informative work-shop from which they will benefit tremendously. After the workshop the top 20% shall be shortlisted and a briefing session will be held pertaining to the rules and regulations of the Final Round. At the end of the session the new products shall be allotted to the teams.

Round 2

The Final round shall be held after the participants have been given a day to prepare their new advertisements.

The Rules to be observed shall be:

• Each team shall have to shoot the advertisement in Aitchison College.

• One day shall be given to the participants to shoot the advertisement in allowed areas.

• The advertisement may only contain shots taken in Aitchison; any other shots may result in disqualification of the advertisement.

• Each Advertisement must be 55-70 seconds long.

• The Advertisement must not degrade any rival product; only promote the assigned one.

• After the advertisement has been displayed the team shall have to provide a verbal synopsis of what they planned to target and achieve through it.

After the Advertisement has been played the team shall be subjected to a 1-minute question and answer session including questions from both the judges and the audience.

The participants’ advertisements shall once again be judged on their creativity, plot as well as their impact and outreach towards the target audience. Further-more, the audio and video editing and the execution of the advertisement shall also contribute to the judges’ decision.

Awards: The top three teams will be awarded with shields.

Wall Street

This year at the Aitchison Business Concept, we are introducing the Wall Street challenge that aims to test the participants and the core of their marketing ex-pertise. This challenge will incorporate three segments of business; the stock market auctioning and individual marketing. while all the other competitions at ACSEC will put your teams business ideas and critical thinking to test, the Wall Street challenge aims to have the participants compete on an individual level. There will only be one winner of this competition and will be declared the Wolf of Wall Street.

Only two members of each team may participate in this event.

The currency used in this event will be ACR.

Event Format

Round 1

In the first round teams will have to set up stalls to sell their product/service. Each team will be provided the following:

  • Custom Currency - *ACR 500 (ACR 10 * 50)
  • 1 Table
  • 1 Board 8ft by 4 ft.
  • Miscellaneous materials to make stalls :Chart paper markers, ribbons etc.
  • ACR= Aitchison College Rupee
  • 2 Chairs

A maximum of four members can take part in this event from each team. The teams will be given 3 hours to decorate their stalls and make them look as attractive as possible with the materials provided by the main stall. The concept of “first come first serve” will be used as there will only be a limited number of products. The team will be given a specific amount of money in ACR which they will have to use carefully as they can only buy a specific number of the above mentioned products and not all. However teams can sell their products to other teams at a higher price and use that money to buy a product that will benefit them. This will not only give them a chance to redecorate their stall if they made a wrong purchase but their thinking and creativity skills will also be put into test and all these transactions will be noted down by a host team member.

There will be no elimination; all teams will go forward to Round 2

Round 2

The judges will examine the stalls of all the teams. 20% of the score would be on the basis of the stalls’ designs, whereas, 80% of the score would be on the basis of Marketing and Presenting. Basically, teams have to market their products in a strategic manner in order to make them appeal to the judges. It is advisable that each team spend about 3- 4 minutes to demonstrate their product to the judges. The top 7 teams from this Round will advance to the Final Round.

Round 3

In this round, the teams will be allotted a concept/idea which they will have to incorporate in their products. Each team will then have come up with a Business Plan Presentation. The Plan should be inclusive in the sense that it should cover points on how the product shall be introduced in the market and how it should be targeted to consumers. All the teams would be required to be as comprehensive as possible within the allotted time frame of 5-8 minutes. Just like Kickstarter, the participants will be expected to raise a certain amount of money to fund their products by pitching their plans to the judges/investors. Since every judge will only have a limited amount of funds, they will have the choice to apportion their funds accordingly. The fun part is that there will be no judging criteria and simply the product that gets funded completely or bags the maximum amount of funding would be declared the Winner. Awards: The winner of this event will be awarded a shield and declared the Wolf of Wall Street.

Business Idea Competition

This year at the Aitchison Business Concept we are continuing our flagship event in which teams must come up with their own business idea. This Business may revolve around a product or a service. On this event teams will showcase sell and then present their ideas.

Event Format

Round 1

In the first round the teams will present their idea to a panel of judges. The team will be judged on their presentation, speaking skills and he feasibility of the product. An ideal presentation should include the following:

  • Problem/Need of Product
  • Introduction of Product
  • Feasibility
  • Target Market
  • Marketing Mix
  • The Offer / Why do you need the money?
  • Swot Analysis
  • Financials
  • Start-Up Cost
  • Exit Strategy

The presentation should be of 6-8 minutes after which there will be a mandatory question and answer question. Teams will be judged on their presentation, feasibility of their idea as well as their presentation skills..

Round 2

The top 25% of the teams from Round 1 will then be selected for Round 2. In this round, the teams will be using the presentation from the previous round but have to also add the following to their presentation:

  • Income Statement
  • Balance Sheet
  • Growth/Expansion Opportunities

The presentation should be of 8-10 minutes after which teams will have a mandatory question and answer session with the judges.

Awards: The top three teams will be awarded with shields.
